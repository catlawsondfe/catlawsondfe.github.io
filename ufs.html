Unit for Future Skills

The Unit for Future Skills aims to improve the quality and availability of data and intelligence on skills and jobs across government.

Contents

  1. Role
  2. Data
  3. Research
  4. Contact

The Unit for Future Skills is an analytical and research unit within the Department for Education. It was set up to improve the quality and availability of jobs and skills data to help identify and understand the skills gaps facing the economy.

The data and research from the Unit for Future Skills will help ensure policy is effective and support government priorities including levelling up and achieving net zero.

Role

Working closely with analysts and data experts across government departments, the Unit for Future Skills will:

  • link datasets across government departments to identify how skills are used in the economy
  • commission research projects to address data gaps and improve our evidence-base
  • create bespoke dashboards to support the further and higher education sectors, Local Skills Improvement Plans and Mayoral Combined Authorities
  • become a centre of expertise on future skills
  • develop robust methodologies that uses existing data to assess current and future skills needs

The unit builds on the analysis and research of the Skills and Productivity Board.

Data

The Unit for Future Skills has produced 3 dashboards to show individualsâ€™ outcomes into employment and future earnings after gaining qualifications.

  1. HE sector destinations allows users to see which industry sectors graduates are employed in, if they are related to their subject of study, and which subject areas lead to quality employment (high earnings) in each sector and region.
  2. 16 to 18 FE sector destinations allows users to see which qualifications lead to employment in which industry sectors. It includes all key stage 5 qualifications in FE colleges.
  3. Career pathways shows which are the most popular qualifications for those in employment in a specific sector within a local area.

Research

The Unit for Future Skills conducts internal and externally commissioned research projects to achieve a deeper understanding of skills matching in the economy.

See the latest research projects.

Contact

If you are interested in the work of the Unit for Future Skills and would like to get in touch, email ufs.contact@education.gov.uk.
