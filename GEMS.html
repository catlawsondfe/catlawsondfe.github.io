Advice for schools, academies and multi-academy trusts to help manage their school buildings, land and equipment.

School leaders, governors and trustees are responsible for maintaining all school buildings, land and equipment. This includes:

  • maintaining your school estate (internally and externally)
  • being responsible for the health and safety of school buildings, land, furniture and equipment
  • dealing with urgent repairs
  • managing your budget so to allow for maintenance work
  • arranging funding for both planned and unplanned (or urgent) maintenance works

This manual provides advice on the policies, processes and documents that will help you manage your estate and the legal requirements and health and safety regulations you must follow.

  1. Overseeing your school estate An overview of strategic estate management and your responsibilities. Includes advice on the governance structure, documents to help manage your estate's performance and polices and procedures you should have in place.
  2. Managing the performance of your buildings and land Creating a budget plan for your estate, managing your budget and using data to measure the performance of your estate.
  3. Health and safety requirements Your legal responsibilities for managing and maintaining school buildings, land, furniture and equipment. Including advice on maintenance checks and testing, how often you should do them and the legal documents you need.
  4. Maintaining your buildings Advice for the person in charge of looking after the school estate on a daily basis to help identify maintenance work and repairs.
  5. Procuring and managing maintenance work Advice on planning and delivering estate projects to improve, maintain or fix your buildings and facilities. Includes information on dealing with emergencies.
